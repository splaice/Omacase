An opinionated Mac, in one command.

Omacase installs, configures, themes, and manages a tidy, tiling macOS setup — all from a single line in your terminal. It borrows Omarchy's spirit (keyboard-first, one consistent look everywhere, fully reproducible) and translates it to the way macOS actually wants to work.

The first rule is simple: don't fight macOS. The result is a calmer, faster, better-looking Mac — one you can roll back to exactly how it was, whenever you like.

What you get
01

One command

Set the whole thing up with a single line — and take it back down just as easily. Re-run it anytime; nothing breaks.

02

Keyboard-driven

Your windows arrange themselves and snap into place. Focus, move, resize, and switch spaces without ever reaching for the mouse.

03

One look, everywhere

Pick a theme and it restyles everything at once — terminal, editor, the status bar, window borders, even macOS Light/Dark and your wallpaper. 19 themes ship in the box, or bring your own.

04

Reversible by design

Before changing a thing, Omacase quietly snapshots your setup. Don't like the result? One command puts your old Mac back.

05

Plays nice with macOS

It leans on Spotlight, the native menu bar, and built-in shortcuts. It trades a little polish for a lot less maintenance — and never picks a fight with the OS.

06

A bar that earns it

A minimal status bar adds only what macOS's own menu bar lacks: live CPU & memory, a software-update nudge, and a keep-awake toggle.

Switch the whole look

The two rules
  1. We don't fight macOS. We compromise polish for ease of maintenance.
  2. It uninstalls just as easily as it installs.
